03 · BOTTLENECK

The bottleneck begins before officer review

Email can carry the application, but it cannot validate whether the package is complete, consistent and ready for review.

ISSUE·01
Inconsistent formats

Formats vary between applicants, requiring officers to interpret each package manually [5]

ISSUE·02
Errors remain undetected

Email has no point-of-entry validation, so omissions may surface only when review begins [5]

ISSUE·03
Clarification chains

Repeated requests and resubmissions can extend timelines and increase uncertainty [5], [20]

OUR OBJECTIVE Detect errors at submission, not during officer review. Less routine checking for IMDA. Fewer correction rounds for applicants.
Sources: [5] IMDA Guidelines on orbital-slot licence applications; [20] Fast Company report on regulatory approval delays for space startups.

10 · PAYOFF

Both sides of the counter win

FOR IMDA
A predictable, auditable workflow
→Strengthens IMDA's standing as an efficient notifying administration — with the ITU and with industry
→Slow approval is a documented barrier for space firms [20] — lowering it grows the ecosystem IMDA and NSAS are mandated to develop [1]
FOR APPLICANTS
Earlier filing = orbital priority
→ITU registration is first-come: earlier filings get protection from later ones [14]
→Predictable timelines cut launch delays and financing costs for firms waiting to launch [1], [20]
→A process firms can trust makes them more willing to bring new missions to IMDA
11 · RISK REGISTER

Two real limitations — and why we proceed anyway

Commercial engine constraints

Engines don't encode Singapore-specific rules — ownership thresholds, NPV-at-10%, insurance [5], [12]

✓SG rules run as separate checks IMDA updates directly
✓Early rollout: engine flags only, officers verify
✓No engine output decides an application — a gap reduces automation coverage, not decision quality
Cost, borne by public funds

Integrations, GovTech onboarding, PDPA-compliant data handling, security upkeep and training are carried even if uptake is slow [15], [16]

✓Phased rollout: MVP delivers highest-value features first
✓Corppass access, role-based permissions, encryption and audit logs protect each filing
✓Automation added only when usage data justifies it — spending follows demonstrated demand
✓The status quo has its own growing cost: officer hours and reputational risk
