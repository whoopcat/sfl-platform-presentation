# Rubrics — what is actually being graded

Three rubrics apply. The **Team Presentation rubric** is the one this deck is scored against;
the **Team Proposal rubric** governs the report the deck draws from; the **Classroom
Engagement rubric** is continuous assessment.

---

## 1. Team Presentation rubric (CA2b) — the one that scores this deck

**Individual effectiveness: 80%** · **Team performance: 20%**

### Content (individual)

Excellent requires:
- All content **highly purposeful, relevant and engaging**
- Sufficient supporting details with **strong and significant connections clearly established**
- Q&A responses evidencing **in-depth** knowledge and understanding

The gap between "Good" and "Excellent" is engagement and the strength of connections —
"content lacks engagement, but is purposeful and relevant" is only a C. Rhetorical questions
and storytelling are named in the student-copy rubric as examples of engaging content.

### Verbal skills (individual)

High competence in spoken grammar and pronunciation · high accuracy in word choice ·
**appropriate register strictly adhered to** · smooth flow throughout, minimal fillers.

### Non-verbal skills (individual)

- Effective use of **vocal strategies** producing sustained audience interest
- Eye contact **well sustained with all parts** of the audience throughout
- Varied facial expressions and gestures to facilitate different messages
- Strong confidence and enthusiasm through body language
- **Use of space and handling of visual aids** confidently and professionally executed

### Team dynamics (team, 20%)

- Positive appearance as a team **including non-presenting members** — the rubric explicitly
  penalises "signs of lack of engagement/interest from non-presenting members"
- **High level of professionalism in handing-over processes**, observed consistently
- **Connections established strongly across sections with effective use of cohesive devices**

> This is why the deck hard-codes five named speaker segments and puts handover instructions
> in the delivery cues. Sections that "appear independent and disjointed" are the Fail
> descriptor.

### Visual aids (team, 20%)

- **Appealing and well-designed**, easy to read and understand
- **All sources appropriately acknowledged; strict adherence to required citation conventions**

> Citation adherence is scored explicitly and is the cheapest criterion to fully satisfy.
> Every on-slide claim must carry its bracket, and the final slide must list all references.

## 2. Team Proposal rubric (CA2a) — governs the report content

**Content and Organisation: 75%** · **Language: 25%**

Excellent-level criteria worth carrying into the pitch:

- **Objectives:** all major *and minor* objectives clearly identified and appropriately
  prioritized.
- **Background / research focus:** guiding research focus *very* clear; necessary and
  sufficient background, both relevant and significant; professional sources providing
  complete depth and breadth; recommended solutions well supported by collected information.
- **Design context:** completely described; problem statement **precisely identified through
  detailed analysis of ideal, gap and goal**.
- **Design thinking is illustrated** — the alternative(s) analysed **for desirability,
  technical feasibility and viability** (this is Table 1 in the report appendix).
- **Benefits:** relevant, significant, and made *exceptionally* clear.
- **Constraints:** all relevant constraints very clearly and fairly identified, **broadly
  addressed** and accurately analysed.
- **Conclusions:** fully relevant and significant, all results/decisions logically derived.
- **Organisation:** logical coherence within and across sections **always exceptionally
  clear**; key concepts, points of view, inferences, **assumptions and implications**
  considered and discussed with exceptional breadth.
- **Language:** no errors; evaluative language expressing **certainty–uncertainty** and
  positive–negative assessment (i.e. hedging is scored — "a target to be validated, not an
  established result" is exactly the register being rewarded); IEEE style clearly and fully
  implemented in-text and end-of-text; diagrams always enhance and clarify.

## 3. Classroom Engagement rubric — continuous

Excellent: always punctual, always on time with assignments, always participates
enthusiastically, **very frequently asks questions and contributes**, takes initiative in
feedback seeking *and* feedback providing, always enacts feedback from peers and teacher,
actively listens, shows utmost respect, high attendance.

Relevant here because **Q&A participation by every member** is assessed, and attentiveness
during other groups' presentations counts.

## Deck design implications

| Rubric line | What the deck must do |
| --- | --- |
| Strict adherence to citation conventions | Bracket every claim; full IEEE list on `s14` |
| Equal airtime / handover professionalism | Five fixed speaker segments; handover cues |
| Engaging, not just relevant | Rhetorical questions, story beats, the before/after flip |
| Design thinking illustrated | Evidence → principle → tier traceability must be visible |
| Hedged, evaluative language | "A target we commit to validating, not assuming" |
| Constraints broadly addressed | The risk register slide, with mitigations *and* residual risk |
| Well-designed, easy to read | No chartjunk, no competing focal points, one idea per beat |
